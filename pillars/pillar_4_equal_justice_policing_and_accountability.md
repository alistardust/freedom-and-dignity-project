## Core Principle
The justice system must protect public safety while applying the law fairly, consistently, and equally to all people. The power of the state to punish must be limited, accountable, humane, and free from systemic bias or exploitation.

## Purpose
This pillar governs how state force, punishment, detention, policing, and legal accountability operate in real life.

## 1. Remove Profit Incentives from Punishment
- Ban private prisons.
- Ban private detention facilities.
- Prohibit financial incentives tied to incarceration or detention levels.
- Extend these rules to contractors and subcontractors that effectively profit from punishment or confinement.

## 2. Sentencing Reform and Consistency
- Eliminate mandatory minimum sentences.
- Establish structured sentencing guidelines:
  - defined ranges,
  - judicial discretion with written justification,
  - reviewable deviations.
- Reduce unjustified disparities in sentencing.
- Address racial inequality in sentencing and enforcement.
- Avoid the contradiction between rigid mandatory minimums and unrestricted judicial discretion by using structured discretion.

## 3. Excessive and Inhumane Punishment
- Abolish or strictly limit the death penalty.
- Earlier user preference included:
  - eliminating the death penalty except possibly in cases of war crimes or crimes against humanity.
- Prohibit cruel, degrading, or disproportionate punishment.
- Maintain public safety without resorting to inhumane state power.

## 4. Forced Labor and the Thirteenth Amendment Exception
- Eliminate the prisoner exception to the Thirteenth Amendment.
- End forced labor in incarceration settings.
- Any prison labor must be:
  - voluntary,
  - fairly compensated,
  - focused on rehabilitation and skill-building.
- Distinguish clearly between voluntary work and exploitative labor.

## 5. Detention, Internment, and Group-Based Punishment
- Ban indefinite detention.
- Ban internment camps and internment-style systems.
- Ban detention based on identity or group status.
- Require individualized due process and judicial oversight.
- Ban offshore incarceration or use of foreign countries to incarcerate American prisoners.
- Ban American prisons on foreign soil designed to evade constitutional accountability.

## 6. Equal Application of the Law
- Standardize data collection on enforcement and sentencing.
- Identify and correct systemic disparities.
- Apply consistent legal standards across cases.
- Ban discriminatory enforcement and selective targeting.
- Ban racial profiling.
- Target over-policing of BIPOC neighborhoods and analogous unequal enforcement patterns.

## 7. Accountability and Oversight
- Independent review mechanisms.
- Transparency in outcomes and processes.
- Clear pathways to challenge unjust treatment.
- Real consequences for misconduct.
- Public trust depends on visible, credible accountability.

## 8. Policing and Public Safety Systems
### A. Reduce Overreliance on Force
- Shift training toward:
  - de-escalation,
  - conflict resolution,
  - community protection.
- Limit routine use of military-style equipment and tactics.
- Demilitarize police.

### B. Specialized Crisis Response
- Establish non-police response teams for:
  - mental health crises,
  - non-violent emergencies,
  - situations police are not trained to handle.
- Ensure coordination where necessary without defaulting every problem to armed police.

### C. Officer Well-Being and Mental Health
- Regular mental health screening for officers.
- Ongoing access to support and counseling.
- Reduce stigma around seeking help.
- Recognize that officer mental health affects community safety and escalation risk.

### D. Community Integration
- Promote community-based policing models.
- Encourage officers to live in or meaningfully engage with the communities they serve.
- Shift police culture away from occupation-style models.

### E. Unequal Enforcement Patterns
- Track and publish data on:
  - stops,
  - arrests,
  - use of force.
- Identify and reduce patterns of over-policing and unequal treatment.

### F. Training Reform
- Expand training in:
  - de-escalation,
  - communication,
  - crisis intervention,
  - service to communities.
- Reduce emphasis on purely tactical or force-based approaches.
- Include de-escalation and service as core professional competencies.

## 9. Public Safety Clarification
- Fairness does not mean leniency.
- Public safety is non-negotiable.
- A just system must:
  - protect the public,
  - hold people accountable,
  - and apply the law consistently.
- Public safety and civil rights are not in conflict; systems that are fair and trusted are more effective.

## 10. Additional Related Protections Discussed
- Ban executive kill lists or “disposition matrix”-style systems without full due process.
- Guarantee due process universally.
- Ensure humane treatment and prevent abuse through legal black holes.
- Protect civil rights as an active enforcement commitment, not just a symbolic principle.

## Pressure-Test Lessons That Must Be Incorporated
- “Soft on crime” attacks must be answered by clearly centering public safety.
- Sentencing reforms must avoid both rigidity and arbitrariness.
- Ending prison labor exploitation requires replacement systems that preserve rehabilitation and dignity.
- Internment bans must be tightly defined to prevent rebranding under other labels.
- Publishing disparity data must be standardized and solution-oriented.
- Private prison bans must include shadow contractor systems.
