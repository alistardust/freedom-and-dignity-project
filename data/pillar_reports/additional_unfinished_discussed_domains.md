# Additional Major Policy Areas Discussed but Not Yet Fully Reconstructed Into Standalone Pillars

> Catalog sync: Imported brainstorm-branch1part2.txt and updated catalog. See data/policy_catalog.sqlite, data/dedupe_report.json, data/full_db_dump.json, and data/intent_report.json. Current counts: 101 policy_items, 1,051 rule_items, 138 record_links (43 approved by Alice), 888 prose mentions.


These items were explicitly discussed in the earlier work and should not be lost. Some were partially integrated into the five core pillars above, but several still deserve fuller reconstruction or their own dedicated pillar(s).

## 1. Courts and Judicial System
- Supreme Court term limits.
- Code of ethics for the Supreme Court and broader judiciary.
- Judicial reform to reduce bad-faith ideological capture.
- Original discussion also recognized that interpretive rules — especially rejection of blind textualism without context and intent — interact closely with judicial reform.
- Possible separation between:
  - rights-interpretation rules,
  - court-structure reforms,
  - ethics and accountability reforms.

## 2. Checks and Balances / Institutional Accountability
- Broader safeguards and guardrails were discussed beyond the executive alone.
- Impeachment and removals:
  - expand list of impeachable conduct,
  - include concrete examples,
  - lower threshold or add secondary mechanism for conviction that still preserves a high bar,
  - possible citizen-triggered impeachment-hearing mechanisms.
- Congressional restoration and oversight reform may deserve a distinct pillar.

## 3. Term Limits and Age-Related Eligibility
- Term limits for judges and elected officials.
- Age limit of 75 for re-election or judicial service was proposed.
- Officials over 70 taking cognitive assessments every six months, with results made public, was proposed.
- This remains unresolved and politically sensitive but was clearly discussed.

## 4. Taxation, Wealth, and Economic Structure
- Taxes must be progressive.
- Prevent wealth hoarding.
- Georgism was discussed as a possible framework.
- A minimum 70% effective tax rate on the top 1% of earners was proposed.
- Later refinement suggested constitutionalizing the principle of anti-oligarchic progressive taxation while leaving exact rates to statute.
- This area likely deserves its own pillar or sub-pillar.

## 5. Healthcare as Standalone Governance Domain
- Healthcare was integrated into rights, but also discussed as a potentially distinct major pillar.
- Prevent partisan interference in coverage decisions.
- Universal healthcare was treated as a core systemic requirement.

## 6. Antitrust and Corporate Power as Standalone Domain
- Strong antitrust rules were discussed enough that they may ultimately deserve their own pillar.
- The concentration of production, supply chains, pricing, quality degradation, and disposability in the economy was part of the broader political diagnosis and messaging.

## 7. Racial Justice / Equal Justice Framing
- “Racial justice” as a label was discussed and then reframed because of political baggage.
- The preferred frame moved toward “Equal Justice & Accountability” or similar language that still directly addresses racial inequality in sentencing, policing, profiling, and enforcement.

## 8. Gun Reform as Potential Standalone Domain
- Gun reform was discussed in enough detail to justify its own later pillar if desired.
- For now it was integrated into Rights, but it remains large and politically explosive enough that a separate document may eventually be better.

## 9. Information Integrity, Disinformation, and Journalism
- Beyond anti-corruption rules, there was discussion of:
  - banning knowing dissemination of false information on behalf of foreign states,
  - allowing civil remedies for organized hateful misinformation,
  - media conflict-of-interest disclosure,
  - the limits of broad misinformation bans,
  - avoiding censorship or political abuse.
- This may ultimately need its own more detailed doctrine document.

## 10. Naming, Movement Architecture, and Distribution
- The project was framed as a larger movement, not just a policy memo.
- Discussion included:
  - one-page movement documents,
  - campaign handouts,
  - 10 demands,
  - detailed pillars,
  - future open-source/project structure,
  - naming and branding,
  - media and communication strategy.
- These are not constitutional pillars but are part of the actual movement architecture.

## 11. Narrative Themes Explicitly Developed
These themes were central to messaging and should not be lost even though they are not formal pillars:
- “This isn’t how it’s supposed to feel.”
- People work harder but fall further behind.
- Ownership is harder to achieve.
- Life is increasingly organized around renting, subscribing, replacing, and dependency.
- Products are increasingly disposable and difficult to repair.
- A smaller number of actors control more of daily life.
- This is not just left vs right; it is about whether the system still works for people at all.
- “A life worth fighting for” became a major narrative frame.
