## Core Principle
No president should be able to act above the law, consolidate unchecked power, or use the office for personal, political, or financial gain.

## Purpose
This pillar is designed to prevent the presidency from becoming a quasi-monarchical office. Executive power must be limited, transparent, reviewable, and fully accountable to law.

## 1. Legal Accountability
- Presidents are explicitly subject to criminal and civil law.
- No immunity for “official acts” that violate the law.
- No self-pardons.
- No pardons for co-conspirators in executive misconduct.
- No pardons for people who protect, conceal, or facilitate presidential wrongdoing.
- Independent prosecutorial mechanism for cases involving the president.
- Safeguards against politically motivated prosecutions, including high evidentiary thresholds and structured review.

## 2. Emergency Powers
- Emergency declarations automatically expire within a fixed window, such as 30–60 days.
- Congress must affirmatively approve any extension.
- Emergency categories must be narrowly defined in law.
- Emergency powers must be tied to the specific category invoked.
- Emergency powers require periodic re-justification and renewal.
- Fast judicial review is automatically available.
- Emergency powers may not be used as a substitute for legislation.
- Emergency flexibility is preserved for genuine crises, but only with strict expiration and review.

## 3. Executive Orders
- Executive orders must cite explicit constitutional or statutory authority.
- Executive orders must include written legal justification.
- Executive orders affecting fundamental rights or large-scale policy shifts:
  - automatically expire unless ratified,
  - or are subject to fast-track review and congressional override.
- Explicit prohibition on using executive orders to effectively legislate without Congress.
- Orders must be narrowly scoped and time-limited when based on emergency authority.
- Generic, continuing, or open-ended executive policymaking is disallowed.

## 4. Financial Conflicts, Self-Dealing, and Presidential Wealth
- Mandatory full divestment or a true blind trust within a fixed timeline.
- Original proposal discussed:
  - all non-divested investments or business ownership stakes are seized and forfeited upon inauguration,
  - with a 30-day grace period,
  - and Congress may extend that grace period by 60 days on a president-by-president basis.
- Non-compliance triggers automatic asset forfeiture, liquidation, or other mandatory enforcement.
- Ban on using office to benefit:
  - self,
  - family,
  - business associates,
  - political donors,
  - or entities in which the president retains concealed interests.
- Ban on using federal policy, contracting, tariffs, taxes, enforcement, or public power to enrich the president or close affiliates.
- Real enforcement backed by civil and criminal penalties.

## 5. Pardon Power Reform
- No self-pardons.
- No pardons for family members in cases involving presidential misconduct.
- No pardons for co-conspirators in executive wrongdoing.
- No blanket or preemptive pardons in defined abuse scenarios.
- Mandatory written justification and public disclosure for all pardons, clemency grants, and commutations.
- Independent review mechanism for abuse.
- Goal: severely limit or remove the ability to use pardon, clemency, or commutation powers for self-protection or political corruption.

## 6. War Powers and Military Authority
- No sustained military action without explicit congressional authorization.
- Ban open-ended authorizations of military force (AUMF-style loopholes).
- Ban continuing or generic authorizations of military force.
- Ban wars that are not officially declared or otherwise specifically authorized within tight constitutional limits.
- All authorizations must include:
  - scope,
  - duration,
  - objectives,
  - and review schedule.
- Automatic funding restrictions for unauthorized actions.
- Strict limits on domestic military deployment.
- Narrow statutory and constitutional limits on domestic use of force, including reforms to emergency and insurrection-related authorities.
- The president’s role with the military must be more limited, especially for unilateral escalation.

## 7. DOJ and Law Enforcement Independence
- Protected independence rules for DOJ in sensitive investigations.
- All White House–DOJ communications in sensitive matters must be logged and reviewable.
- Removal of key law-enforcement or oversight officials requires cause and documentation.
- Criminal penalties for improper interference.
- Explicit ban on:
  - targeting political opponents,
  - shielding allies,
  - or manipulating prosecutorial and investigative outcomes for partisan or personal reasons.
- The Attorney General and relevant officials operate under legally defined independence standards in investigations involving the president or senior officials.

## 8. Appointments and Senate Confirmation
- Strict time limits for acting officials.
- No rotating titles or reassignment tricks to bypass Senate confirmation.
- Clearly defined positions requiring Senate confirmation.
- No repeated reassignment loopholes.
- Key offices become vacant if confirmation deadlines are not met.
- “Acting” service cannot become a long-term workaround for advice-and-consent requirements.

## 9. Duty to Faithfully Execute the Law
- The president cannot nullify laws through systematic non-enforcement.
- Mechanism for challenge when laws are intentionally and systematically ignored.
- Congress’s power to legislate is protected from executive nullification-by-inaction.
- Courts or designated oversight mechanisms can intervene when systematic refusal to enforce laws occurs.

## 10. Secrecy and Classification
- Ban classification to conceal illegality, abuse, corruption, or misconduct.
- Independent classification review authority.
- Time limits and automatic declassification review.
- Protected whistleblower channels for classification abuse.
- Secrecy cannot be used as a shield against accountability.

## 11. Surveillance and Intelligence Powers
- Warrant requirements for domestic surveillance.
- Explicit ban on political targeting or surveillance of opponents.
- Independent auditing authority with real enforcement power.
- No “national security” loophole for domestic abuse.
- Intelligence powers cannot be used to bypass constitutional protections.
- Strong protections against hidden domestic surveillance architectures.

## 12. Anti-Retaliation and Civil Service Protection
- Protect officials who refuse unlawful orders.
- Strengthen whistleblower protections.
- Require cause for removal of oversight officials.
- Ban loyalty tests for lawful civil service performance.
- Explicitly state that presidents may not fire people for political reasons where the purpose is to obstruct oversight, punish lawful conduct, or consolidate personal power.

## 13. Separation of Official Duties and Political Activity
- Ban use of federal resources for campaign or personal gain.
- Clear penalties for mixing governing with political operations.
- Recordkeeping and disclosure requirements for contacts, travel, and resource use in borderline cases.
- Prevent turning the federal government into a campaign machine.

## 14. Budget, Spending, and Impoundment
- President must execute appropriated funds.
- Strict limits on withholding, delaying, or redirecting funds.
- Transparency and automatic review triggers.
- Reinforce Congress’s constitutional power of the purse.
- Prevent impoundment-style abuse or budget sabotage by technicality.

## 15. Domestic Use of Force
- Strict limits on domestic military or federal force deployment.
- Narrow definition of emergency use.
- Mandatory reporting plus rapid review.
- Strong civil-liberties protections.
- Domestic force cannot be normalized as a tool of political control.

## 16. Records, Transparency, and Public Accountability
- Constitutional baseline for presidential records.
- Mandatory preservation and disclosure.
- Criminal penalties for destruction, concealment, or unlawful withholding.
- Fast disclosure mechanisms in constitutional crises.
- The base framework of the Presidential Records Act should be constitutionalized, with implementation details in statute.

## Enforcement Backbone
- Independent oversight body.
- Automatic triggers for investigation.
- Fast-track judicial review.
- Public transparency requirements.
- Hard constraints rather than soft norms.
- Automatic expiration, automatic review, automatic disclosure, and automatic enforcement triggers wherever possible.

## Pressure-Test Lessons That Must Be Incorporated
- Avoid relying on good faith.
- Use hard constraints instead of norms.
- Build automatic triggers into the system.
- Preserve emergency flexibility without creating loopholes for authoritarian accumulation.
- Include safeguards against weaponized prosecutions while still ending immunity.
- Prevent bypass through informal influence, shadow chains of command, or administrative technicalities.
