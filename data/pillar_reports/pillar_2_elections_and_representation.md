## Core Principle
Every eligible person must have equal access to voting, equal weight in representation, and confidence in election outcomes.

## Purpose
This pillar is the architecture of democratic legitimacy. It must ensure that people can vote, that their votes are counted fairly, that representation is not manipulated, and that election outcomes cannot be sabotaged by partisan actors.

## 1. Constitutional Right to Vote
- Establish an explicit constitutional right to vote.
- Strong federal protection against suppression.
- Prevent arbitrary or discriminatory barriers.
- Ensure equal protection of voting access across all states.
- Codify core voting-rights protections into the Constitution.

## 2. Equal and Practical Access to Voting
- Automatic voter registration.
- Same-day voter registration.
- Early voting protections.
- Mail voting protections.
- National minimum standards for ballot access.
- Polling infrastructure standards:
  - maximum voter-to-polling-place ratios,
  - measures to prevent excessive wait times,
  - practical standards rather than symbolic compliance.
- Ability to vote at alternate locations if a normal precinct location fails or becomes inaccessible.
- Use of provisional or verified ballots at alternate locations to prevent disenfranchisement caused by administrative breakdown.

## 3. Voting Access for Working People
- Election Day should be a national holiday.
- Employers must provide paid time off to vote.
- If a worker must work on Election Day, paid time off must include access to early voting.
- Workers must be protected from retaliation for taking time to vote.

## 4. Election Security and Auditability
- Voter-verifiable paper ballots.
- Routine post-election audits.
- Strong chain-of-custody standards.
- Transparent and reviewable election procedures.
- Independent election oversight bodies.
- Actual security and auditability must be guaranteed rather than merely asserted.

## 5. Election Administration and Certification
- Clear, neutral rules for election administration.
- Strong protections against partisan interference in counting, certification, recounts, or administrative processes.
- Protect election workers and administrators from intimidation or retaliation.
- Certification safeguards:
  - automatic certification if legal requirements are met,
  - penalties for unlawful refusal or delay,
  - backup certification mechanisms if officials fail to act.
- Fast legal triggers when certification is obstructed.

## 6. Gerrymandering Reform
- Ban partisan gerrymandering.
- Independent redistricting commissions with strict conflict-of-interest rules.
- Required mapping criteria:
  - equal population,
  - compactness,
  - contiguity,
  - preservation of communities of interest,
  - prohibition of partisan bias.
- Algorithmic or neutral baseline maps generated using transparent methods.
- Statistical fairness testing, including rejection of partisan outlier maps.
- Automatic judicial review of maps before use, not years later.
- Explore additional methods to make gerrymandering harder through population-density and geographic standards.

## 7. Equal Representation and Vote Weight
- Reduce structural vote inequality.
- Move toward population-based fairness.
- Address systems that cause some votes or voters to carry more weight than others.
- Original discussion included:
  - eliminating the Electoral College,
  - tightening district population equality,
  - exploring systems that better reflect real voter distribution,
  - reducing scenarios where some votes count more than others.
- Equal representation must mean both access and equitable weight.

## 8. Direct Democracy and Citizen Participation
- States should be required to allow voter-initiated ballot initiatives, referendums, or petition mechanisms.
- Standardize petition access.
- Reduce unnecessary procedural barriers to public participation.
- Improve transparency around initiative campaigns and funding.
- Ensure direct-democracy tools are not captured by moneyed interests without disclosure.

## 9. Voter Roll Protections
- Prevent improper purging.
- Transparent voter-roll maintenance systems.
- Notification and appeal rights before removal.
- Prevent federal overreach regarding voter rolls while still preserving integrity safeguards.
- Administrative list maintenance cannot become a suppression tool.

## 10. Accessibility and Inclusion
- Disability access.
- Language access.
- Rural access.
- Access for underserved communities.
- Voting rights must be practical, not just theoretical.

## 11. Candidate Eligibility and Insurrection Disqualification
- Standardized candidate-eligibility review process.
- Explicit enforcement mechanism for the constitutional ban on insurrectionists holding office.
- Challenges may be initiated by:
  - election officials,
  - courts,
  - qualified challengers meeting evidentiary thresholds.
- Expedited judicial review before ballots are finalized.
- Automatic removal from ballots when disqualified.
- Penalties for frivolous or bad-faith challenges.

## 12. Citizenship and Birthright Citizenship
- Make the Fourteenth Amendment much more explicit about birthright citizenship.
- Reduce room for ideological or bad-faith reinterpretation.

## Enforcement Backbone
- Federal baseline standards with real enforcement authority.
- Automatic audits and oversight triggers.
- Fast-track judicial review for election disputes.
- Legal recourse mechanisms for voters.
- Criminal penalties for intentional interference or unlawful obstruction of election processes.

## Additional Reforms Discussed and Flagged
- Eliminate the Electoral College.
- Restore voting rights more broadly, including after incarceration.
- Explicit protection of election certification from partisan sabotage.
- Ranked-choice voting and proportional systems were discussed as possible future representation reforms.
- Senate malapportionment and related distortions were recognized as major structural issues, though not fully developed.

## Pressure-Test Lessons That Must Be Incorporated
- Access and security must be treated as complementary, not conflicting.
- National holiday alone is not enough; it must be paired with paid time off.
- Cross-precinct or alternate-site voting must avoid administrative chaos through verification and provisional-ballot systems.
- Polling-place standards require enforceable funding or federal support.
- “Independent” commissions can still be captured unless process safeguards are robust.
- Ballot initiatives can be captured by money unless anti-corruption rules apply.
- Election rules must prevent administrative failure from becoming disenfranchisement.
