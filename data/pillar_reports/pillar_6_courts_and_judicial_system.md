## Core Principle
The judiciary must be independent, accountable, ethically bound, and structurally designed to uphold rights, not undermine them.

## Purpose
Define how courts operate, how judges are selected and constrained, and how interpretation of law and rights is governed.

## Key Areas (To Be Fully Developed)
- Supreme Court term limits
- Judicial term limits across all levels
- Mandatory code of ethics (binding and enforceable)
- Recusal standards and enforcement
- Mechanisms for removal or discipline of judges
- Transparency in judicial decision-making
- Rules of constitutional interpretation:
  - role of intent
  - role of context
  - limits of textualism
- Judicial review scope and limits
- Lower court structure and reform
- Preventing ideological capture of courts

## Open Questions
- How to enforce ethics without politicizing the judiciary?
- How to balance independence with accountability?
- How to structure appointments and confirmations to reduce extremism?
