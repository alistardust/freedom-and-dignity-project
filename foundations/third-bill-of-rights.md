# A Third Bill of Rights

## The Unfinished Project

The First Bill of Rights told government what it could not do to you.

Ten amendments. Freedom of speech, of assembly, of religion. The right to due process, to a fair trial, to be free from unreasonable search and seizure. Ratified in 1791. Essential. Still contested. Still unfinished.

Franklin Roosevelt saw that they were not enough.

On January 11, 1944 — in the middle of a world war, with the Great Depression still in living memory — he stood before Congress and said:

> *"True individual freedom cannot exist without economic security and independence."*

> *"Necessitous men are not free men."*

He proposed a Second Bill of Rights — eight guarantees that would make freedom real for ordinary Americans:

1. The right to a useful and remunerative job
2. The right to earn enough for food, clothing, and recreation
3. The right of every farmer to a decent living
4. The right to freedom from unfair competition and monopoly
5. The right to a decent home
6. The right to adequate medical care and good health
7. The right to protection from the economic fears of old age, sickness, and unemployment
8. The right to a good education

Congress never passed them. Roosevelt died sixteen months later. The Cold War made economic rights easy to attack as socialism. The window closed.

Eighty years later, those rights remain unfinished — and the world has changed in ways Roosevelt could not have imagined.

---

## What the First Two Bills Left Undone

The First Bill of Rights did not mention privacy in the digital age, because there was no digital age. It did not protect bodily autonomy explicitly, because women were not full citizens when it was written. It did not address algorithmic control, mass surveillance, or the right to information free from systematic manipulation.

Roosevelt's Second Bill did not address climate — the right to breathe clean air, drink clean water, and inherit a livable planet. It did not address immigration dignity — the right to basic legal protection regardless of origin. It did not address the new forms of concentrated power that now shape daily life: platforms, algorithms, AI systems that make consequential decisions about people's lives without transparency or accountability.

The unfinished work is large.

## A Third Bill of Rights

> *"You can't be free if you're sick, homeless, or in debt."*

This project is built on the conviction that the time has come to finish what was started — and to extend it into the century we actually live in.

A Third Bill of Rights, completing FDR's and extending beyond it:

### Completing the Second Bill

- **The right to healthcare** — access to medically necessary care without financial ruin
- **The right to a living wage** — indexed to inflation and productivity
- **The right to housing** — freedom from arbitrary displacement and exploitative conditions
- **The right to education** — without debt servitude
- **The right to economic security** — protection from want in old age, disability, and hardship
- **The right to a fair economy** — freedom from monopoly, wage theft, and predatory practices

### Rights for the World We Live In

- **The right to privacy** — including digital life, medical records, and personal data; freedom from surveillance capitalism and mass government tracking
- **The right to bodily autonomy** — reproductive rights, gender-affirming care, freedom from non-consensual medical intervention
- **The right to algorithmic accountability** — no consequential decision about your life made by an opaque automated system without explanation, appeal, and human accountability
- **The right to a livable environment** — clean air, clean water, and a stable climate as prerequisites for all other rights
- **The right to human dignity regardless of origin** — basic legal protection and fair process for every person within the jurisdiction of this government
- **The right to information integrity** — a press free from political retaliation; protection from coordinated mass disinformation; the right to know when you are being manipulated
- **The right to safety from weapons of war** — government authority to regulate firearms, ban weapons designed for mass casualties, and prohibit private armies

### Rights of Democratic Self-Governance

- **The right to vote** — explicit, constitutional, enforceable
- **The right to a government free from corruption** — elected officials who answer to constituents, not donors
- **The right to equal justice** — the same law applied the same way regardless of wealth, race, or status
- **The right to accountable power** — no president, judge, or official above the law; no permanent ruling class; no institution exempt from democratic oversight

## How This Project Is Organized

The five foundations of this project are the rights categories of a Third Bill of Rights:

| Foundation | The Right |
|---|---|
| **Accountable Power** | The right to a government constrained by law |
| **Clean Democracy** | The right to a government that answers to people |
| **Equal Justice** | The right to equal treatment under law |
| **Real Freedom** | The right to live without coercion, surveillance, or weapons of war |
| **Freedom to Thrive** | The right to the material conditions that make freedom real |

The 17 policy pillars are the implementation — how each right gets translated into enforceable rules, institutional design, and structural reform.

---

## The Argument

This is not a radical project. It is the continuation of a tradition as old as the republic — the conviction that rights must evolve as the world evolves, that freedom is not just the absence of chains but the presence of real conditions for a dignified life.

The First Bill of Rights was written when only some people were considered fully human. It was extended, imperfectly and incompletely, through two centuries of struggle. Roosevelt recognized that it was not enough for a world of industrial capitalism and economic insecurity. We now recognize that it is not enough for a world of algorithmic power, climate crisis, and systematic erosion of the institutions that make self-governance possible.

The work is not finished. It has never been finished. This project is the next chapter.
