## Key decisions made in this session

- **No anchor crisis framing.** The charter does not frame ESRP around a single
  crisis. There are many pressure points; the research follows the evidence.

- **No separate repo specification in the charter.** The charter no longer prescribes
  where research outputs live — that's operational detail. For now, outputs go in
  `policy/research/esrp/` in the main repo.

- **First contributor role is open, not filled.** The charter describes an ideal
  profile (reading/synthesis skills, domain knowledge in land use, ecology, labor
  economics, or public finance) but makes no assumption about who fills it or when.

- **Adversarial review is a continuous loop, not a phase gate.** Every substantive
  output goes through the review cycle before being marked `reviewed`. The audit log
  is the record — not "AI reviewed this."
