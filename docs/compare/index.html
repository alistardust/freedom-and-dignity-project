How does Freedom and Dignity's structural framework relate to the platforms of America's major parties and movements? These comparisons examine overlaps, divergences, and coverage gaps — not to rank or endorse, but to map the terrain honestly.

About These Comparisons

A Non-Partisan Framework

The Freedom and Dignity Project is not a political party. It is a constitutional and structural reform framework — completing the unfinished work of FDR's Second Bill of Rights as a Third Bill of Rights. It operates across 22 pillars spanning governance, democracy, justice, rights, and economic conditions.

These comparisons examine how existing parties and movements address — or fail to address — those same 22 pillars. Where coverage gaps exist, they are named plainly. Where a party's depth exceeds Freedom and Dignity's current detail, that is acknowledged too. The goal is an honest map of the landscape, not a scorecard.

Freedom and Dignity is neither left nor right. It is a framework for durable structural repair. Every party below addresses some of what Freedom and Dignity addresses — and leaves other parts untouched.
