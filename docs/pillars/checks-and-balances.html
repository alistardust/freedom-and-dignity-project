Foundation I: Accountable Power

Checks & Balances

★ ★ ★

The system of institutional limits on power must be rebuilt to withstand authoritarian pressure.

Why This Matters

The American system of government was designed with the assumption that each branch would jealously guard its own power. That assumption has failed. Congress has ceded power to the executive for decades.

What It Demands

  • Strengthen inspector general independence and anti-removal protections
  • Enforce contempt of Congress through criminal prosecution, not just civil action
  • Restore legislative prerogatives ceded to the executive branch over the past 50 years

What It Rejects

  • Congressional abdication of war powers, spending authority, and oversight
  • Executive agencies that operate as political arms of the White House
  • Judicial deference to executive power as a default

Key Policy Points

  1. Legislative independence from executive coercion
  2. Inspector general protections
  3. Contempt of Congress with real consequences
