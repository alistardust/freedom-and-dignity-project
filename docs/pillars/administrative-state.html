Foundation I: Accountable Power

Administrative State

★ ★ ★

Federal agencies must serve the public, not the industries they are supposed to regulate.

Why This Matters

The regulatory apparatus exists to protect the public. When agency heads are drawn from the industries they regulate and return to those industries afterward, they are structurally unable to do that job.

What It Demands

  • Five-year cooling-off period for all senior agency officials before joining regulated industries
  • Whistleblower protections that apply to all federal employees, including contractors
  • Public comment requirements with mandatory agency response for all major rulemakings

What It Rejects

  • The revolving door as an accepted feature of government service
  • Political appointees using agency authority to benefit their future employers
  • Regulatory capture masquerading as deregulation

Key Policy Points

  1. Revolving door restrictions
  2. Agency independence from political interference
  3. Rulemaking transparency and accountability
