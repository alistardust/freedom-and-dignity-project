Foundation III: Equal Justice

Rights & Civil Liberties

★ ★ ★

Civil rights enforcement must match the scale and sophistication of modern discrimination.

Why This Matters

Discrimination has not ended — it has evolved. Modern discrimination is algorithmic, structural, and deniable. Civil rights law written in the 1960s was not designed to address mortgage algorithms, hiring software, or social media targeting.

What It Demands

  • Restore and strengthen the Voting Rights Act with updated preclearance requirements
  • Federal non-discrimination protections for LGBTQ+ individuals in employment, housing, and public accommodations
  • Fund and staff the EEOC and DOJ Civil Rights Division to match the actual scale of discrimination

What It Rejects

  • The fiction that the Civil Rights Act solved discrimination
  • Algorithmic discrimination as a loophole around anti-discrimination law
  • Civil rights enforcement as a political choice rather than a legal obligation

Key Policy Points

  1. Strengthen the Voting Rights Act
  2. LGBTQ+ non-discrimination protections
  3. Disability rights enforcement
