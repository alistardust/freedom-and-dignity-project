Foundation V: Freedom to Thrive

Taxation & Wealth

★ ★ ★

The tax system must reward work, not inheritance. Extreme concentration of wealth is incompatible with democracy.

Why This Matters

The top 1% now own more wealth than the entire middle class combined. The tax code has been systematically rewritten to protect and compound that concentration. A democratic society cannot function when economic power is this unevenly distributed.

What It Demands

  • Annual wealth tax of 1-2% on fortunes over $50 million, 3% over $1 billion
  • Tax capital gains at the same rate as ordinary income for households earning over $400,000
  • Eliminate the stepped-up basis at death, which allows unrealized gains to pass tax-free across generations

What It Rejects

  • A tax code where billionaires pay lower effective rates than their secretaries
  • Inheritance as a mechanism for creating a permanent hereditary aristocracy
  • The carried interest loophole and other provisions with no purpose other than enriching the already-wealthy

Key Policy Points

  1. Wealth tax on extreme fortunes
  2. Capital gains taxed as ordinary income
  3. End of stepped-up basis loophole
