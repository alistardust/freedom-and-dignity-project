Foundation II: Clean Democracy

Anti-Corruption

★ ★ ★

Public office must not be a path to private enrichment.

Why This Matters

Corruption in American politics is not primarily the dramatic bribery of film — it is the structural corruption of revolving doors, dark money, and stock trades that turn legislative knowledge into personal profit.

What It Demands

  • Complete ban on stock trading by members of Congress and their immediate families
  • Mandatory disclosure of all political contributions over $200, including LLCs and pass-through entities
  • Criminal penalties for officials who take positions at companies they regulated within two years of departure

What It Rejects

  • Dark money as a legitimate vehicle for political influence
  • Stock trading by officials with access to non-public legislative information
  • A culture where public service is understood as a prelude to private enrichment

Key Policy Points

  1. Disclosure of all political spending
  2. Congressional stock trading ban
  3. Post-government employment restrictions
