Foundation II: Clean Democracy

Antitrust & Corporate Power

★ ★ ★

Monopoly power corrupts markets and democracy alike. It must be broken up.

Why This Matters

The United States has not meaningfully enforced antitrust law for forty years. The result is an economy where a handful of companies control the food supply, healthcare delivery, media, banking, and technology — and the political system that might regulate them.

What It Demands

  • Restore the original intent of the Sherman Act: market dominance itself is the problem, not just provable consumer harm
  • Break up agricultural conglomerates controlling more than 25% of any food category
  • Apply antitrust law to labor markets: prosecute employer collusion to suppress wages

What It Rejects

  • The consumer welfare standard as the sole test of antitrust harm
  • Mergers that eliminate competition in exchange for efficiency gains that never materialize
  • The idea that four companies controlling the food supply is compatible with a free market

Key Policy Points

  1. Aggressive antitrust enforcement
  2. Breaking up media monopolies
  3. Labor market concentration rules
