Foundation V: Freedom to Thrive

Healthcare

★ ★ ★

Healthcare is a right. No one should go bankrupt, go without, or die because they couldn't afford care.

Why This Matters

The United States spends more on healthcare than any other wealthy nation and achieves worse outcomes by almost every measure. The reason is not a mystery: we have organized healthcare as a profit-generating industry rather than a public good.

What It Demands

  • Universal healthcare coverage — no gaps, no networks, no prior authorizations for medically necessary care
  • Medicare drug price negotiation extended to all medications, with price caps aligned to peer nations
  • Full mental health and substance use parity: same coverage, same cost-sharing, same access as physical health

What It Rejects

  • Medical bankruptcy as an acceptable outcome in a wealthy nation
  • Drug pricing that charges Americans three to ten times what other countries pay for the same medications
  • A mental health system so underfunded that most Americans cannot access care when they need it

Key Policy Points

  1. Universal coverage
  2. Drug price negotiation
  3. Mental health parity
