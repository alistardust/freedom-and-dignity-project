Foundation V: Freedom to Thrive

Environment & Agriculture

★ ★ ★

A livable planet and a food supply that is clean, safe, and fairly produced are non-negotiable.

Why This Matters

Climate change is not a future problem — it is the defining crisis of this century, already reshaping where people can live, what they can eat, and how severe the next disaster will be. Simultaneously, four companies now control most of the American food supply, and the farmers who grow that food are being squeezed out of existence.

What It Demands

  • Carbon pricing that makes the true cost of fossil fuels visible in the market, with revenue returned to households
  • Antitrust enforcement in agriculture: break up the four-company consolidation of meat, grain, and seed markets
  • Fully fund and enforce the Clean Water Act; prosecute industrial polluters, not just fine them

What It Rejects

  • Environmental deregulation framed as economic freedom when it is simply the socialization of pollution costs
  • An agricultural system optimized for the profit margins of three or four companies at the expense of farmers, communities, and eaters
  • Climate denial or delay as policy

Key Policy Points

  1. Carbon pricing and clean energy transition
  2. Family farm protections
  3. Clean water enforcement
