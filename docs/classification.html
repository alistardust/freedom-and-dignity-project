Policy Classification & Gap Analysis

A systematic audit of 1,095 canonical policy rules: how each is implemented, what the platform is missing, and what a genuine Third Bill of Rights still needs to address.

Methodology. Every rule was classified by the minimum legal mechanism required to implement it under current U.S. constitutional law. Amendment: requires Article V ratification (2/3 Congress + 3/4 states). Statute: enacted by simple majority of Congress. Policy: executive order, agency rulemaking, or procurement requirement — no congressional vote required but reversible. State: primarily within state jurisdiction under the 10th Amendment.

Classifications are based on the canonical rule statement and represent the minimum mechanism; some statute-classified rules would benefit from constitutional grounding for durability. The DB reflects the pre-expansion state (July 2025). Three pillars (Housing, Consumer Rights, Legislative Reform) have content in markdown pending DB reimport and are not reflected in these counts.

Amendment-Required Rules: Priority List

Of the 65 rules requiring constitutional amendment, the highest-priority for near-term advocacy are those with the broadest public support and the most structural impact:

  • ELE-REP-001 — Abolish the Electoral College (popular vote for President)
  • ELE-VOT-001 — Explicit constitutional right to vote (currently implied, not guaranteed)
  • TRM-LIM-001/002/003 — Congressional term limits (high public support across party lines)
  • RGT-PRV-001 — Explicit right to privacy (foundation of digital rights agenda)
  • RGT-EQT-001 — Expanded equality protections (complete the 14th Amendment)
  • JUD-ETH-001 — Supreme Court term limits (Art. III "good behaviour" requires amendment)
  • COR-FIN-001/002 — Campaign finance reform (requires overturning Citizens United via amendment or a reconstituted Court — tracked as "amendment or statute, Court-dependent")

FDR's Second Bill of Rights: Fulfillment Audit

This platform explicitly positions itself as completing FDR's unfinished 1944 agenda.[1] The eight rights FDR proposed, and how the current DB covers them:

# FDR's Proposed Right (1944) Current Coverage Assessment
1 Right to a useful and remunerative job ECO-LAB-004, LAB-WRK (4 rules total) ⚠ Weak
2 Right to earn enough for adequate food and clothing ECO-EQT-001 (equal pay); no living wage rule ⚠ Weak
3 Right of every family to a decent home HOU pillar — 0 DB rules (markdown pending reimport) ✗ Gap
4 Right to adequate medical care HLT — 184 rules across full healthcare spectrum ✓ Strong
5 Right to a good education EDU — 2 DB rules (markdown has ~40+ pending reimport) ⚠ Weak (DB)
6 Right of every businessman to trade free from unfair competition COR-FIN (4 rules); no dedicated antitrust scope ~ Partial
7 Right of every family to protection from monopolistic practices Same as above; no ATR- scope code formalized ~ Partial
8 Right to protection from economic fear of old age, sickness, accident, unemployment Partial in HLT-COV, ECO-AUT; no Social Security reform pillar ~ Partial

Verdict: The platform fully covers one of FDR's eight rights (medical care), partially covers three, is weak on two, and has a critical gap in one (housing). As a "Third Bill of Rights," the platform needs to address housing and economic security at the same level of rigor as healthcare and immigration.

Gap Analysis

The following gaps were identified through adversarial review: reading the platform as a critic would, looking for what is absent, what is claimed but underdeveloped, and what a working-class or rural voter would find missing.

Critical Gaps — Policy areas missing or near-absent

Critical
Foreign Policy & National Security
No dedicated pillar, no scope code, zero rules. GOV-WAR (3 rules) touches war powers but does not address diplomatic strategy, alliance obligations (NATO), intelligence oversight, sanctions, arms control, or America's role in international institutions. A "Third Bill of Rights" platform that is silent on foreign policy is structurally incomplete.
Critical
Veterans & Military Families
No dedicated pillar, no scope code, zero rules. TEC-MIL (58 rules) covers AI in military systems but not veteran healthcare, VA reform, PTSD treatment, toxic exposure coverage (burn pits), GI Bill modernization, or military family support. The platform speaks to TEC but not to the humans who serve.
Critical
Gun Policy — Pillar Exists, Rules Don't
A dedicated gun_policy pillar exists in HTML but has only 2 cross-scope rules in the DB (JUS-POL-006, JUS-POL-007). No GUN- scope code. No background check standards, no assault weapons policy, no red flag law framework, no safe storage mandates. The platform claims this ground but has not formalized it. This is the most visible credibility gap.
Critical
Housing — Pillar With Zero DB Rules
The HOU pillar has zero DB rules. Markdown content exists in pillars/housing/policy.md but has not been reimported to the database. This is FDR's Third Right — the right to a decent home — and it is the largest gap between the platform's stated ambition and its formalized policy corpus. The DB reimport must be a top priority.
Critical
Consumer Rights — Pillar With Zero DB Rules
The CON/RPR pillar has zero DB rules. No formalized policy on financial consumer protection, predatory lending, debt collection, product liability, food safety, or data broker regulation. Markdown content exists pending reimport.
Critical
Agricultural Policy — 1 Rule for a Nation of Farmers
Only AGR-REG-001 (promote regenerative agriculture) exists in the DB. No rules on farm subsidy reform, food security, rural economic development, SNAP/food assistance, agricultural labor, agribusiness concentration, or rural broadband. The platform's rural credibility depends on expanding this scope significantly.

Major Gaps — Present but seriously underdeveloped

Major
Antitrust & Corporate Power
Dedicated pillar exists but no ATR- scope code. Only 4 cross-scope rules (COR-FIN) address corporate power, and these focus on campaign finance, not market competition. No rules on monopoly breakup authority, merger review standards, Big Tech platform antitrust, pharmaceutical market concentration, or Amazon/Google/Meta-scale market power.
Major
Disability Rights
Only 3 rules tangentially related (RGT-IDN) and some HLT-ACC coverage. No ADA modernization, no accessibility mandates for digital services, no disability income support, no voting accessibility framework, no independent living support policy. Disability rights are the largest unaddressed civil rights domain in the platform.
Major
Tribal & Indigenous Rights
Zero identifiable rules for sovereign tribal nations, treaty obligations, Missing and Murdered Indigenous Women (MMIW), BIA reform, water rights, or tribal land sovereignty. A platform claiming comprehensive rights coverage while ignoring the First Peoples is a credibility failure, not a minor omission.
Major
Reparations
No rules, no pillar, no explicit policy position. Whether or not the platform endorses reparations for slavery and Jim Crow, the complete absence of any engagement with the question is conspicuous for a platform positioning itself as addressing structural racial inequality and completing FDR's unfinished agenda.
Major
Financial Regulation & Banking
ECO covers automation taxation and some insurance rules. No rules on banking regulation, CFPB authority and independence, predatory lending, payday loans, credit reporting reform, student loan servicer accountability, or systemic financial risk. The 2008 financial crisis was the largest economic event of the past generation; it has no policy response in this platform.
Major
DC Statehood & Territories
Only ELE-REP-003 (territories get House representation). No rules on DC statehood, full voting rights for Puerto Rico, Guam, USVI, or American Samoa, or territorial self-determination. Approximately 3.6 million Americans in U.S. territories have no voting representation in Congress.[2]
Major
International Agreements & Treaties
No rules on treaty obligations, multilateral agreements, trade policy, climate treaty compliance, WHO/WTO/UN participation, or international human rights law. The platform's silence on America's role in international governance is a structural gap for a document designed to modernize democratic governance.
Major
Drug Policy Reform
JUS-DRG has only 5 rules. No comprehensive decriminalization framework, no marijuana legalization or regulation policy, no harm reduction mandate, no safe injection site policy, no drug court expansion framework, no treatment-over-incarceration mandate. The War on Drugs has been the single largest driver of mass incarceration; 5 rules is not a serious response.

Minor Gaps — Covered but could be expanded

Minor
Social Security & Retirement Security
Partial coverage in HLT-COV and ECO-AUT. No dedicated framework for Social Security solvency, pension protection, elder care, or retirement security reform. The original FDR agenda centered on protection from economic fear of old age.
Minor
Climate Change
ENV (9 rules) covers pollution and biodiversity. No dedicated climate framework: no carbon pricing mechanism, no net-zero mandate, no climate adaptation or resilience policy, no just transition program for fossil fuel workers. The climate section is significantly outweighed by the AI/tech section (361 rules vs. 9).
Minor
Reproductive Rights Depth
RGT-BOD (8 rules) covers bodily autonomy, abortion, and contraception — a meaningful foundation. Gaps: IVF access and embryo rights, maternal mortality (the U.S. has the highest rate among high-income nations[3]), pregnancy discrimination, and parental leave beyond ECO-LAB-004.
Minor
Childcare & Early Education
EDU (in DB) focuses on K-12 and higher ed. No pre-K universal access rules, no childcare subsidy framework, no Head Start expansion policy. Childcare access is one of the largest barriers to women's workforce participation.
Minor
Legislative Reform — Pillar With Zero DB Rules
LEG pillar has 0 DB rules. Markdown pending. No rules on filibuster reform, unanimous consent abuse, congressional capacity (staff resources, CBO independence), or congressional ethics. The body that is supposed to pass most of this platform has no reform framework in the current DB.

Recommendations

P1
DB Reimport — immediate. Run scripts/import_policy_catalog.py to import Housing, Consumer Rights, Legislative Reform, Education (expanded), and Labor (expanded) from markdown. This will bring the DB into alignment with the actual state of the platform and close three zero-rule pillars.
P2
Formalize Gun Policy. Create a GUN- scope code. The gun_policy markdown already exists; it needs a formal import pass. Minimum viable DB content: background checks, assault weapons/high-capacity magazines, red flag laws, safe storage. This is the most visible credibility gap for a platform claiming 22 pillars.
P3
Formalize Antitrust. Create an ATR- scope code. The antitrust_and_corporate_power markdown exists; import with at minimum: platform monopoly regulation, merger review standards, pharmaceutical market concentration, and media ownership concentration rules.
P4
Add Foreign Policy & National Security pillar. Create a FPS- scope code. Minimum viable content: war powers reform (building on GOV-WAR), intelligence oversight, multilateral treaty obligations, NATO/alliance commitments, arms control. The platform's silence on foreign policy is a structural gap for a governing framework.
P5
Add Veterans & Military Families pillar. Create a VET- scope code. Minimum: VA healthcare reform, burn pit/toxic exposure coverage, PTSD treatment mandates, GI Bill modernization, military family economic support. This closes a major gap and broadens the platform's appeal to veterans communities.
P6
Add Tribal & Indigenous Rights. Create a TRB- scope code. Minimum: treaty enforcement, tribal sovereignty, BIA reform, MMIW response, water rights, land rights. The platform cannot credibly claim comprehensive rights coverage while ignoring the rights of sovereign Indigenous nations.
P7
Expand Agricultural Policy. Expand AGR from 1 to at least 15 rules. Priority areas: farm subsidy reform, food security guarantees, rural economic development, agribusiness concentration, agricultural labor protections, rural broadband access. This is essential for rural political credibility.
P8
Add Financial Regulation framework. Create ECO-FIN or a new FIN- scope. Minimum: CFPB authority and independence, banking regulation, predatory lending prohibition, consumer credit reform, systemic financial risk oversight. The 2008 financial crisis was not a minor event.
P9
Expand Drug Policy. Expand JUS-DRG from 5 to at minimum 15 rules: decriminalization framework, marijuana regulation, harm reduction mandates, drug court expansion, addiction treatment as healthcare. The War on Drugs has been the primary engine of mass incarceration for 50 years.
P10
Prioritize amendment campaign strategy. Of the 65 amendment-required rules, focus early organizing on Electoral College abolition (ELE-REP-001), explicit right to vote (ELE-VOT-001), congressional term limits (TRM-LIM), and right to privacy (RGT-PRV-001) — these have the broadest bipartisan public support and the clearest structural impact.
