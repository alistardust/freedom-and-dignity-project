How We Fix This

The Plan

The problems are structural. That means the fixes have to be structural too — and structural change is possible. Here's how we get there.

The Strategy

Five Steps. In Order. No Shortcuts.

Sustainable reform requires building in the right sequence. You cannot fix the outcomes without fixing the mechanisms that produce them. This is the sequence.

  1. 1

    Fix the Amendment Process

    The U.S. Constitution requires a two-thirds supermajority of both houses of Congress and ratification by three-fourths of states to amend. In today's political environment, that is effectively impossible. Without this fix, every reform we win through legislation can be dismantled overnight. Structural change has to be structurally durable.

  2. 2

    Reform Impeachment and Removal

    Accountability is not optional. When officials can commit abuses of power and remain in office because the removal process is captured by party loyalty, accountability becomes theater. Real accountability means real consequences — and a removal process that works when it matters most.

  3. 3

    Reform the Judiciary

    An independent judiciary is the backstop for every other right and protection. When courts are packed for outcomes instead of selected for independence, that backstop disappears. Restoring judicial independence means removing the incentive to capture courts and rebuilding the norms that protect them.

  4. 4

    End Corruption and Lobbying Capture

    Policy that is bought is not democratic. As long as organized money can purchase legislative outcomes, every other reform operates upstream of the problem. Ending the capture mechanism — through contribution limits, revolving-door rules, and enforceable transparency — is how you make the other reforms stick.

  5. 5

    Elect and Pressure Reform Leaders

    Structural reform requires political power. That means identifying, electing, and consistently pressuring officials at every level — state legislatures, Congress, the executive branch — who will carry these reforms forward. The platform is the standard. The pressure is sustained. The coalition crosses party lines.

Where We Are

Early Days. Real Work.

We are not going to tell you we are further along than we are. Here is an honest accounting.

The Platform

The policy platform is live and actively growing — five structural foundations, more than 20 pillars, and hundreds of specific policy positions. It is a working document, not a finished product. Positions are proposed, reviewed, and revised in public.

The Community

A small, serious group of people is doing the early work: writing policy, sourcing evidence, reviewing proposals, building the infrastructure. The community is open. Every person who joins makes it stronger and more representative.

The Coalition

We are at the coalition-building stage. The work right now is reaching people who share these structural commitments — regardless of which party they currently belong to or which label they use for themselves. That is the foundation everything else is built on.

The Movement

The electoral and organizing work comes next. Building a movement that can sustain pressure across election cycles requires the foundation the platform and community provide. We are building that foundation now.

Your Part

There Is No Single Way to Do This

Some people organize. Some run for office. Some review policy proposals. Some shift the conversation in their community one person at a time. Some contribute five hours a week. Some contribute five minutes. All of it counts. Here are a few of the roles that matter.

Organize Locally

Connect with neighbors, coworkers, and community members around these structural issues. Start conversations. Host discussions. Build the local relationships that movement politics runs on. No credentials required — just the willingness to show up.

Shift the Conversation

Share the platform. Talk about structural reform — not just policy outcomes, but why the system is producing those outcomes. Help people see the problem clearly and the solution credibly. One conversation changes minds in ways no advertisement can.

Review Policy

Every policy position in this platform is a proposal open to scrutiny. If you have expertise in healthcare, housing, labor, environmental law, criminal justice, education, or any other domain — we need your review. The standard is adversarial: we want people to challenge the proposals, not just validate them.

Run for Office

The platform is a public resource. Any candidate at any level who shares these structural commitments can run on this platform, adapt it, and use it. School board. State legislature. Congress. There is no permission required to run for office and make this case to voters.

Contribute Your Skills

Writers, researchers, designers, technologists, lawyers, organizers, translators — if you have a skill that builds movements or sharpens ideas, there is a place for it here. The platform is open source. So is the work.

Fund the Work

Infrastructure takes resources. Keeping this platform free, open, and independent requires ongoing support. If you believe in the work and have the means, your contribution makes it sustainable for people who don't.

The door is open. Come in at whatever level you can.
