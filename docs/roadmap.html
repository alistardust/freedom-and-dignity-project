Project Roadmap

Where we are, what comes next, and the work still ahead — across policy development, organization, outreach, and technical infrastructure.

The Freedom and Dignity Project is a living platform — built in the open, subject to revision, and committed to intellectual honesty. This roadmap tracks eight parallel work streams: policy content, organizational formation, outreach, fundraising, content and branding, technical infrastructure, quality assurance, and ongoing research. Statuses reflect the current state as of April 2026. No date promises are made; milestones are criteria-based.

Version Milestones

Milestones define concrete, verifiable criteria — not calendar dates. A milestone is reached when every criterion is met, not before. "Pre-1.0" means the platform is published and useful but not yet complete; the WIP banner will remain active until v1.0.

02 Organization Not Started

Phase 1 — Legal Prerequisites (must complete first)

  • ○ Finalize platform name — "Freedom and Dignity Project" is a working title; trademark search required before public launch
  • ○ Engage legal counsel with nonprofit and 501(c)(4) experience
  • ○ Establish fiscal sponsorship with an aligned organization (e.g., Tides Advocacy, NEO Philanthropy) — enables legal receipt of donations immediately; legally required before any public fundraising
  • ○ Open organizational bank account under fiscal sponsor umbrella

Phase 2 — Governance Structure

  • ○ Define decision-making process — consensus, advisory board, or weighted voting; must be documented before decisions are made on behalf of the organization
  • ○ Establish founding board or steering committee — minimum three members; diverse in background and geography
  • ○ Draft board bylaws — including quorum, voting, term lengths, removal procedures
  • ○ Adopt conflict of interest policy — required for IRS tax-exempt application; must cover board members and key staff
  • ○ Adopt volunteer contributor agreement — covers IP assignment, code of conduct, and confidentiality for all contributors

Phase 3 — Legal Entity Formation

  • ○ File articles of incorporation — structure (501(c)(4) vs. 501(c)(3)) to be determined by legal counsel based on planned activities
  • ○ Apply for federal tax-exempt status — IRS Form 1024 for 501(c)(4); Form 1023 for 501(c)(3)
  • ○ Transition from fiscal sponsorship to standalone entity once IRS recognition received
  • ○ Formalize intellectual property assignment — all contributed work (code, content, design) to be assigned to the organization entity

04 Fundraising Deferred

Deferred — not yet active. Fundraising is an eventual necessity and a legitimate goal for any platform that wants to operate at scale. The project will need resources to grow. But pursuing fundraising before the right foundation is in place would be both legally risky and strategically premature. This track activates after three prerequisites are met: (1) the project has an active contributor base and is no longer solely one person's effort; (2) formal legal organization is established; and (3) the platform name is finalized and trademark protections are secured.

Prerequisites (all must be complete before this track activates)

  • ⊘ Active contributor base — multiple people working on the platform, not a solo project
  • ⊘ Formal legal organization established (fiscal sponsorship or 501(c)(4)) — soliciting donations without a legal entity violates state charitable solicitation laws in most jurisdictions
  • ⊘ Platform name finalized and trademark registered — fundraising under a working-title placeholder creates unnecessary legal and brand risk

Donation ethics policy (to be adopted before first solicitation)

  • ◆ No corporate donations, with one exception: employee-organized workplace giving programs (e.g., a company's employee charitable giving campaign where individual employees direct their own contributions). Corporate PAC money, direct corporate gifts, and bundled corporate contributions are prohibited.
  • ◆ No PAC funding of any kind
  • ◆ Individual donor concentration cap — no single donor to exceed a defined percentage of annual operating budget
  • ◆ Full public disclosure of all donors above a defined threshold; annual financial report published on the website; 990 published publicly once tax-exempt status is obtained

Infrastructure & strategy (future)

  • ◆ Small-dollar online infrastructure — nonprofit-appropriate platforms (Donorbox, Anedot, or equivalent). Note: ActBlue serves candidate campaigns and political committees, not 501(c)(4) nonprofits.
  • ◆ Grant research — progressive and civic foundations aligned with platform goals (NEO Philanthropy, Democracy Fund, Open Society Foundations, and others)
  • ◆ Crowdfunding for specific projects — expert review panels, public events, translation work — once fiscal sponsorship umbrella is in place

07 Quality Assurance & Human Review Not Started

Why this track exists

  • ○ This platform was substantially drafted with AI assistance. The project's own AI transparency policy requires that AI-generated content be treated as a draft until reviewed by human contributors. This track formalizes that commitment into a verifiable process.

Review Pipeline

  • ○ Establish proposal review workflow: all rules currently in the database are flagged as "proposals" until reviewed and confirmed by a human subject-matter contributor; reviewed rules are promoted to "canon" status
  • ○ Subject-matter reviewer recruitment — identify and engage at least one qualified reviewer per pillar area (lawyer, academic, practitioner, or directly affected community member)
  • ○ Factual claim verification pipeline — every statistic, poll result, legal reference, and court case citation in policy HTML must be independently verified against a primary source; unverified claims must be removed or reframed
  • ○ External expert review — recruit domain experts (academic, legal, practitioner) for each pillar; document reviewer credentials and any conflicts of interest
  • ○ Staged copyediting pipeline: contributor draft → policy review → factual verification → copyedit → publish; no content to be marked "canon" until it has passed all stages
  • ○ AI content audit — systematic review of all AI-generated content for hallucinations, misattributions, anachronistic data, and overconfident framing; document findings and corrections
  • ◆ Periodic full-site accuracy review — annual minimum; triggered additionally by major court decisions, new legislation, or significant new research in a pillar area
08 Research & Evidence Base Not Started
  • ○ Ongoing source monitoring — establish a process for tracking updates from BLS, Census, CBO, CRS, GAO, and other primary data sources whose figures are cited in platform content
  • ○ Data update schedule — define how frequently statistics in policy pages are refreshed; assign ownership per pillar; flag stale data
  • ○ Academic partnership program — formalize relationships with public policy, economics, and political science researchers who can validate or critique platform positions with peer-reviewed evidence
  • ○ Comparative policy research — document evidence from peer democracies (UK, Canada, Germany, Nordic countries, etc.) for each pillar; what has been tried, what works, and what has failed
  • ○ Adversarial counterargument documentation — for each major platform position, document the strongest good-faith counterarguments and this platform's response; model intellectual honesty and avoid strawmanning opponents
  • ◆ Research publication pipeline — produce policy white papers and working papers derived from platform research; submit to open-access repositories; build academic credibility over time
  • ◆ Policy simulation and modeling tools — explore partnerships with academic groups for evidence-based impact estimates on major proposed reforms (e.g., fiscal impact, coverage expansion, distributional effects)

Future / Post-1.0

The following are aspirational items with no committed timeline. They represent the long-term potential of this work if it achieves public traction and organizational sustainability. None of these are prerequisites for v1.0.

  • Internationalization — platform adapted for non-U.S. democracies; comparative editions for Canada, UK, Australia, and others
  • State-level adaptations — versions of the platform scoped to state constitutions and state-level policy reform; useful for state legislative campaigns
  • Open data portal and API — full public access to the policy catalog for researchers, journalists, civic technologists, and coalition partners building on this framework
  • Academic research partnership program — formal research collaboration agreements; platform as a living dataset for political science and public policy scholarship
  • Coalition-branded platforms — allied organizations adapting this framework under their own branding for their constituencies
  • Policy simulation tools — publicly accessible impact modeling for major proposed reforms; fiscal, health, environmental, and equity outcomes
  • Multilingual editions — beyond the initial Spanish translation; languages prioritized by reach and coalition need
