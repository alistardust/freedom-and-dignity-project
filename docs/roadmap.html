Freedom and Dignity Project

Project Roadmap

A phased plan to turn the project from a collection of documents into a guided public system centered on PolicyOS, clear proposals, and real participation.

The Freedom and Dignity Project does not need more top-level concepts right now. It needs stronger information architecture. The immediate goal is to make the site feel like a guided path into a system rather than a collection of important ideas. The throughline is simple: Vision → Problem → Approach → Invitation.

00 Strategic Direction Active

The site should guide people through a sequence: a clear vision, an honest account of the problem, a structured explanation of the approach, and a concrete invitation to help.

  • → Pages must be directive, not merely descriptive
  • → Every major page must create a natural next step
  • → PolicyOS must move from a side topic to the centerpiece of the explanation
  • → Proposals must read as outputs of one coherent system

Milestone Mapping

Milestones are phase-based, not date-based. The current priority is an information architecture reset that makes the site understandable before it becomes more expansive.

Milestone Focus Key Criteria Status
1 Information architecture reset + PolicyOS framing Rewrite the roadmap and public structure around Vision → Problem → Approach → Invitation; define the new page roles; make PolicyOS central in the public explanation Current
2 Public-facing approach and proposals structure Publish a clear Approach / PolicyOS page, establish a proposals hub, and use layered summaries so visitors can understand the system before diving into the full documents Planned
3 Contribution funnel + infrastructure support Make Get Involved actionable, add search and collaboration support, and evaluate hosting or internal review tooling only after the public narrative is coherent Planned

The New Bill of Rights track

One naming decision remains open before final public framing: A New Bill of Rights, The People's Bill of Rights, or The Freedom and Dignity Bill of Rights.

# Amendment One-line
1 Right to Vote Your voice in who governs you is sacred and may not be denied, suppressed, or made practically impossible.
2 Right to Self-Governance You have the right to shape the institutions and systems that shape your life.
3 Right to Organize You have the right to join together with coworkers, neighbors, or fellow citizens to demand better.
4 Right to Bodily Autonomy Your body is yours, including your reproductive choices, your medical decisions, and your right to age and die with dignity.
5 Right to Privacy Your personal life, your data, and your communications belong to you, not to corporations or government agencies.
6 Right to Indigenous Sovereignty The nations that were here first have rights that predate this government, and this government has obligations it has not kept.
7 Right to a Healthy Environment Clean air, clean water, healthy waterways and ecosystems, and a stable climate are shared conditions of life.
8 Right to Equal Justice The law applies equally to everyone, and every person has the right to participate fully in public life.
9 Right to Cultural Identity Every person has the right to their language, their faith, their heritage, and their cultural life.
10 Right to Basic Necessities Healthcare, housing, food, a living income, education, childcare, elder care, economic security, and rest are the floor of a free society.

06 Target Site Structure Guiding Spec

Top navigation

Home · Problem · Approach · Proposals · Get Involved · Roadmap

These are the core path pages, not the entire site map. Supporting pages should remain available outside the main narrative flow.

Page roles

  • ✓ Home: orient, inspire, and point directly to the approach
  • ✓ Problem: explain what is broken and why normal politics is not fixing it
  • ✓ Approach: explain PolicyOS and how structure turns values into governance
  • ✓ Proposals: present rights and reforms as outputs of the system
  • ✓ Get Involved: convert interest into concrete participation
  • ✓ Roadmap: show visible progress through phases and milestones

Supporting pages to retain

  • ✓ Letter from the Founder: personal voice, motivation, and political origin story
  • ✓ About Us: who is building the project and what kind of effort it is
  • ✓ AI Disclosure: transparency, safeguards, and limits on how AI is used in the work
  • ✓ Comparison pages: keep them as contextual pages that show how this platform differs from other parties or frameworks after the core project is understood

How comparison pages should be reframed

  • ◆ Update the comparison pages so they support the main site narrative instead of acting as a parallel front door
  • ◆ Compare systems, tradeoffs, and governing assumptions rather than reducing the pages to scorekeeping
  • ◆ Use them after the core explanation, not in place of explaining the project on its own terms

What Success Looks Like

A new visitor should be able to say, quickly and confidently: this is a project to redesign governance using a structured system, and there is a real way for me to help. If the site cannot communicate that, more content will not solve the underlying problem.

  • Legible — the project makes sense fast
  • Directed — each page points to the next step
  • Distinctive — PolicyOS is clearly understood as the differentiator
  • Joinable — contribution paths are visible and specific
  • Scalable — later infrastructure supports the narrative instead of obscuring it
