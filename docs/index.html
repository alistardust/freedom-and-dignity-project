You're Not Wrong. You're Not Alone.

The system has made things genuinely harder. Wages haven't kept up with productivity in decades. Medical debt is the leading cause of bankruptcy. The gap between the rules for the powerful and the rules for everyone else keeps growing — and it's not by accident.[1]

But this isn't inevitable, and it isn't permanent. Every one of those gaps is a structural problem — which means it has a structural fix. That's where we start.

It Doesn't Have to Be This Way.

Other countries have solved these problems. Other generations have solved problems this hard. We are not out of options — we are out of political will, and political will is something people can build. Here are five rights worth fighting for right now.

Five Rights Worth Fighting For

The Rights That Change Everything

Amendment X

You have a right to healthcare, housing, and a living wage.

Medical debt is the leading cause of personal bankruptcy in the United States.[1] Half of Americans can't cover a $400 emergency.[2] These outcomes don't happen in a vacuum — they're the result of policy choices that can be unmade.

Read the policy → See the plan →
Amendment I

You have a right to vote — and have it counted.

Voter suppression laws, partisan gerrymandering, and dark money flooding elections have made it systematically harder for ordinary people to have a meaningful say in who represents them.

Read the policy → See the plan →
Amendment VIII

You have a right to equal treatment under the law.

In America, wealth determines legal outcomes in ways that have nothing to do with guilt or innocence. The same offense carries different consequences depending on your zip code, your race, and what you can afford to pay an attorney.

Read the policy → See the plan →
Amendment IV

You have a right to make your own medical decisions.

Bans on reproductive care, criminalization of gender-affirming treatment, and legislative interference in private medical decisions represent government overreach into the most personal choices a person makes.

Read the policy → See the plan →
Amendment VII

You have a right to clean air, clean water, and a livable climate.

Fossil fuel lobbying has blocked meaningful climate action for decades. The communities bearing the worst environmental burden are overwhelmingly low-income and communities of color — environmental harm is not distributed equally.
