# Three Foundational Rights Documents

This directory contains the three co-equal foundational rights documents of the Freedom and Dignity Project. Each is a standalone document with its own legal and historical register, its own floor and duty statements, and its own full policy architecture.

---

## The Documents

### [New Bill of Rights](new-bill-of-rights.md)
10 amendments — rights for every person in America.

A deliberate parallel to the original Bill of Rights: 10 amendments that extend the unfinished work of 1791 and 1944 into the century we actually live in. These rights apply to every person, regardless of status, income, or identity.

### [New Bill of Workers' Rights](workers-rights.md)
10 rights — because labor built this country and deserves better than it got.

A standalone rights document in the tradition of FDR's proposed Second Bill of Rights. These rights apply specifically in the economic and employment sphere, and are distinct from — and stacked on top of — the guarantees of the main Bill of Rights.

### [Declaration of Indigenous Rights](indigenous-rights.md)
10 rights — because these rights were never given. They were always there, and always violated.

A declaration, not a bill: Indigenous sovereignty predates this government and is recognized, not granted. This document addresses the specific obligations of the United States to Indigenous nations, including Native Hawaiians, Pacific Islander peoples, and the COFA nations.

---

## Why Three Documents

These are not companion documents. They are not appendices. They are three co-equal, fully supported, standalone foundational rights documents, each addressing a distinct legal and historical register:

- **Bill** — for rights asserted within the constitutional framework (Rights, Workers' Rights)
- **Declaration** — for rights that predate the Constitution and must be recognized, not granted (Indigenous Rights)

Each document carries the same weight. Each gets full floor + duty treatment under the PolicyOS model. Each will have its own page on the platform with equal prominence.

---

## The Floor + Duty Model

All three documents apply the PolicyOS floor + duty model to every right:

- **Floor** — the minimum that government may not violate, erode, or fall beneath. A rule fails if it crosses the floor.
- **Duty** — the positive obligation that government must actively secure, build, preserve, and advance. A rule is incomplete if it avoids violation but does not satisfy the duty.

These are not aspirations. They are requirements.
